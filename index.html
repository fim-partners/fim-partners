Firm Information

Overview

FIM Partners KSA is the wholly owned subsidiary of Frontier Investment Management Partners Ltd. It is regulated by the Capital Market Authority (18193-32) (CR 1010475987) with SAR 5,000,000 of paid up capital. FIM Partners KSA has been licensed since the 5th of August 2018 for managing investments. The organisational structure of the firm can be found here. The audited financials of the firm (in arabic) can be found here.

FIM Partners KSA offers investment management services to private non-real estate funds and sophisticated investment portfolios for public equities traded in KSA, drawing on the broader investment experience and track record of its parent company who specializes in emerging and frontier markets.

Senior Management & Board

Mark Heaney, CEO and Board Member

Mark Heaney is the CEO of FIM Partners KSA. Mark has over 25 years’ experience in the investment management industry. Previously, Mark worked at Goldman Sachs Asset Management (GSAM) for 11 years where he was Head of the International Funds Group and a Board Director on the GSAM Fund Boards in Luxembourg & Ireland. Prior to GSAM Mark spent 7 years at Invesco in a number of roles leading to Director of Operations, and was also a Board Director on the Invesco Fund and Management Company Boards in Luxembourg & Ireland. Mark has a B.A. in Accounting from the University of Ulster, and is a member of the Chartered Institute of Management Accountants.

Hedi Ben Mlouka, Chairman and Board Member

Hedi Ben Mlouka is the CIO of FIM Partners overseeing all investment activity of the group. The funds he manages and co-manages have won several awards and nominations including a top ranking on Citywire for the MENA fund‘s 3 Year Performance. Prior to founding FIM Partners in 2008, Hedi was a Director at Merrill Lynch responsible for CEEMEA region for equity and equity derivatives where he was instrumental in developing the first structured transactions on frontier markets. Prior to Merrill Lynch, Hedi worked for BNP Paribas. Hedi holds a M.Sc. in Finance and Economics from the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE).

Karim Moussa, Board Member

Karim Moussa is the head of asset management and private equity for EFG Hermes. Karim is also Co-CEO of EFG Hermes Investment Bank with responsibility for the entire buy-side business of the group. Karim sits on the Investment Committee of several EFG Hermes' sponsored funds and on InfraMed's Investors Board, with combined AUM of c. USD3.5bn. He is also a Member of the Board of Directors of various portfolio companies. Prior to joining EFG Hermes, Karim was a Vice President at Deutsche Bank, in the Global Banking division, with responsibilities for M&A, ECM and DCM advisory in MENA. Karim holds a Masters in Business Administration and Mechanical Engineering (Diplom Wirtschaftsingenieur) from the Technical University of Berlin.

Complaints

Click here to contact the compliance department or for complaints handling procedures. FIM Partners KSA undertakes to handle all complaints received from its customers in the most appropriate manner, and within the shortest time period possible, according to the following mechanism:-

A Customer shall have the right to file a complaint against any of the officers or employees of the firm by submitting the complaint directly to the Compliance Department of the firm or by sending it via registered mail or e-mail at the addresses below to the attention of the Compliance Officer:

The Compliance Officer shall record any such complaints in a register designated by the Company for that purpose.

The Compliance Officer shall proceed to investigate the complaint immediately upon receiving it. He shall examine the complaint in an unbiased way and take the necessary corrective actions, if any, and notify the Customer of the outcome of investigations within a maximum period of four weeks where reasonably practicable.

If the complaint relates to the conduct of a third party working with the firm or recommended by the firm, then the firm will take reasonable steps in the Customer’s interest to resolve the complaint.
